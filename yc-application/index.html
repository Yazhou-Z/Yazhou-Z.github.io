Describe what your company does in 50 characters or less.
Develop AI based wearable ultrasound patch.
What is your company going to make? Please describe your product and what it does or will do.
We develop 1) a wearable ultrasound patch for continuous human tissue imaging, including heart, blood vessels, and liver; 2) AI algorithms for wearable ultrasound image-denoising; and 3) a platform for data analysis, trend alerts, and secure data transmission. The patch is 5 grams (lighter than a single ear AirPods); it will be attached to human skin with hydrogel and monitor the heart, blood clots, or fatty liver. The Al-embedded platform will analyze the data, report the trend, or send alerts to the users. With the patient's permission, the data will be sent to doctors for further use.

Idea
Why did you pick this idea to work on? Do you have domain expertise in this area? How do you know people need what you're making?
Domain expertise: Yazhou (PhD student at NYU) has 4 years of experience collaborating with clinical doctors and has built strong connections with healthcare professionals. Xinyu (PhD student at HKU) has 8 years of experience in materials science and electrode design. We both have over 4 years of background in AI and healthcare. How do you know people need what you're making? We interviewed patients, doctors, patient families, and individuals recovering from conditions with blood clot risks. Many patients with heart conditions express a strong desire for real-time monitoring. They often ask, “Is my heart okay today?” Patients with dilated cardiomyopathy even greet each other with, “How large is your heart today?” And for patients recovering from cardiac surgery often feel uncertain about their recovery progress. They need a real-time system to tell them that their recovery is on track. Post-surgical and bedridden patients fear undetected clots. Fatty liver patients want to get feedback on lifestyle changes (e.g., "Did this diet reduce liver fat?"). Current ultrasound workflows are time-consuming (1+ hour appointments) and uncomfortable. Elderly or rural patients struggle with access to imaging centers. We pick this idea also because it addresses challenges that affect billions of people. Potential users' disease: Dilated Cardiomyopathy (DCM): Affects 1 in 250 individuals. Mitral Valve Insufficiency: Over 175 million people worldwide, including 5 million in the U.S., suffer from conditions like rheumatic mitral regurgitation. Post-Cardiac Surgery Monitoring: Annually, 1 to 1.5 million cardiac surgical procedures (e.g., valve replacements, heart transplants) are performed globally. Venous Thromboembolism (Blood Clots): Approximately 900,000 people are affected in the U.S. each year, with around 100,000 deaths. Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD): Currently affects 32% of the global adult population, a figure expected to exceed 55% by 2040. If untreated, it can progress to non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH), cirrhosis, or even liver cancer. Timely monitoring is crucial because lifestyle changes can lead to rapid improvements.

Please describe the scientific basis for your product. How does it work?
We have a wearable ultrasound patch with sparse array attached to the human skin and can scan the tissue with continuous imaging. Inside the circuit, there is a high-voltage generator, AC switcher, PZT, battery, and BLE to transfer the data to the phone. The figure will be shown by the end of the demo video. Regarding the algorithm, we used AI algorithms to assist in denoising and disease detection. Here is an example solution using generative model to denoise: https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0293468 We are also working on other algorithms (LLM) for better performance.

Does your product need regulatory approval (i.e., FDA)? If so, what is the approval process? What is your plan to get approved, and how much time and money will it take?
Yes. We need FDA and NMPA (in the Chinese market). For the FDA, it is a class 2 medical device. We want to apply for 510 K. It will take ~2 months for file setup, 1 month for FDA acceptance review, ~2 months for substantive review, and 1~6 months for potential additional review. It will take 150k USD. For NMPA, it is also a class 2 device. We plan to apply NMPA after the FDA. It could be faster, it will take less than 9 months and 55k USD.
